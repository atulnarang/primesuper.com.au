There are a couple of easy steps to follow to ensure your account remains 'active' and your super keeps working hard for you at Prime Super.

  • New employer?

  • or

New employer?

If you've moved to a new employer, they may have set up another account for you with another super fund.

It's easy to fix this and bring all of your super back together into your Prime Super account.

Simply download and fill out the slip below and give it to your employer.

Please pay my super into Prime Super slip.

Transfer your super in

If your new employer has already paid into a new account, it's easy to transfer this money into your Prime Super account.

Simply use our Fetch my super tool which will not only identify your new super account, but any other super accounts you may also have.

We'll then help you transfer all of your money into your Prime Super account.

If you can't access the tool online, download and fill out the Fetch my super form below and send it to Prime Super.

Fetch my super form
No longer working?

If you have temporarily left the workforce, you can still keep your account 'active'* so it stays with Prime Super and continues to work hard for you.

This is very important if you have a low account balance. Find out your balance here – or call us on 1800 675 839.

If you have a balance below $2,000 and don't conduct certain transactions (see below) on your account, it could in time, be transferred to the ATO. This means you will lose your insurance cover and investment strategy.

Consider...

If you have a low account balance, you can provide your consent for your super to never be transferred to the ATO. Simply complete the form below and return it to Prime Super.

I'd like to keep my super with Prime Super form

*To keep your account 'active' under Fund rules, you must make a contribution or rollover into your account within a two year period. For an account to be considered 'active' under the ATO definition, you must have transacted on your account within a five year period. Such transactions, in addition to rollovers and contributions, can include making an investment choice, nominating a beneficiary or increasing your level of insurance cover.
