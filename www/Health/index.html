Why choice is important

Before joining just any superannuation plan take a closer look at what’s important to you and what’s on offer from a variety of funds. After all, it’s your money and your future!

Things to compare include:

  • Fees: Low fees will be an important factor in your choice. A difference of even one per cent in fees a year can significantly impact your bottom line come retirement.
  • Performance: Look for good past performance in the medium to longer term (say a fund that has performed well over the last 5 years). While past performance is no guarantee of future performance, it is worth a look at how various funds have performed over time compared to the average.
  • Insurance: Insurance is an important part of your super. All super fund’s default (MySuper) product must provide minimum levels of at least death cover life insurance. However, depending on your super fund, you may also be eligible for total and permanent disability cover and income protection insurance. Premiums will usually be deducted straight from your super account and in some cases without having to undergo a medical check. You will need to contact individual funds for specific details of their insurance offer.
  • Investment choice: Different funds will provide a different range of options. Look for options that suit your needs (both now and in the future) and comfort level with risk.
  • Service: The specifics of super can get complicated and it can be good to know that help is available if you need it. Check to see what other services funds’ offer before choosing.

Prime Super provides a checklist to help you prioritise what you want from a super fund. Download the Super choice checklist.

Nominating a super fund

Unless you are paid under a specific industry award or workplace agreement you have the ability to choose the super fund your employer pays your super into (that is you don’t have to go with your employer’s default fund).

When you have considered what’s on offer from a few different funds and have selected the right fund for you, you must complete an ATO Standard Choice Form and provide it to your employer.

Do you know where your super is?

Once you have chosen the fund that is right for you, you may wish to consider putting all your super in one place.

Benefits of having your super in one place include only paying one set of fees and having only one account to keep track of. One account also makes it easier to manage your contributions, investment strategy and insurance arrangements.

Before rolling over, check whether your other fund will charge exit/withdrawal fees. Also consider how your level of insurance cover will be affected.

Unlike some super funds, we don’t charge you to transfer your super into Prime Super.

You have a few options to roll in:

  1. Prime Super members can roll in online via their MemberOnline account

    If you have the details of the other fund/s you wish to transfer, you can provide us with the details through your online account. Login to your account here, or if you don’t have an account as yet, setting up an online account is easy to do.

  2. Prime Super members can consent to ‘Fetch my Super’ and let us do all the work for you

    Simply login to the secure section, click on the "Fetch my super" link, then follow the prompts. We will look for the super accounts held in your name and transfer them into your Prime Super account on your behalf.

    If you don't have access to the secure site, or you've forgotten your password, you can fix this via the login page.

    Any questions, call us on 1800 675 839.

  3. Alternatively, complete a paper form

    Simply complete the Rollover your super form, send it to us and we’ll do everything for you.
